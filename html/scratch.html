AN ODE TO SIMPLE WEBSITES

Simple websites embody clarity, functionality, and understated elegance. Berkshire Hathaway’s website exemplifies this philosophy. Unlike modern, overly complex sites filled with flashy graphics, Berkshire Hathaway's site is refreshingly minimalistic. Its straightforward design reflects the company’s values—unpretentiousness, focus, and efficiency—qualities central to Warren Buffett's investment strategy and the firm’s reputation as the best in the world. The site features a simple layout with clear links and concise information, making navigation seamless. This sleek, user-friendly approach shows that less can be more, creating a space where investors and visitors can access key information without distraction. It’s a reminder that sophistication doesn’t need complexity. Berkshire Hathaway’s website proves that a simple, well-executed design can inspire trust and communicate confidence in the firm's timeless principles.


Why simple websites are better

  1. They decrease overhead costs for servers and maintenance
    • They also eliminate the need for a full time web programming employee

  2. They allow for simple navigation of the webpage
    • I.e. they are more user friendly

  3. As above, they follow the example of the great Berkshire Hathaway
